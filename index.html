Eutrophication is the process in which a body of water becomes
enriched with nutrients and stimulates the growth of aquatic plant
life (Webster). When eutrophication is present in a body of water,
an algal bloom can form. An algal bloom is when there is a large
accumulation of algae in a body of water (CDC). When algae dies
in the water, they absorb all oxygen present in the area of the
bloom. This creates a deadly hypoxic area called a "dead zone."

Eutrophication in water is caused mainly by fossil fuel burning.
When carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gasses get dissolved
in the water, they attract nitrogen and phosphorus by changing
the weather patterns.

A reverse osmosis system is used to filter out the
nitrogen and phosphorus in the water. The reverse
osmosis mechanism involves a very small pipe to exert
pressure in order to extract nitrogen and phosphorus.
When a small semi-permeable membrane such as proteins
or phospholipids is combined with saline water and
nutrient-rich water, reverse osmosis takes place.
Reverse osmosis is a process in which solids are
extracted from a liquid through a semipermeable
membrane. After the water has been filtered from
the nitrogen and phosphorus, The nutrients get sent
to a nutrient chamber where they sit there until the
machine is cleaned.

Nutrient pollution and eutrophication negatively impacts
aquatic organisms, aquatic industries, the economy, and
humans. These impacts all originate from algal blooms. In
this section, only US waters will be mentioned for making
impacts.

Dead zones caused by algal blooms kill thousands of aquatic
animals every single year. Dead zones like the Gulf of
Mexico dead zone is responsible for 72% of fish deaths
in US waters (carleton). The Gulf of Mexico supplies 80%
of seafood used in the US including shrimp, oysters, and
commercial fish (carleton). A reduction in the amount of
fish due to hypoxic areas can greatly impact the number
of imported seafood to the US. Reduction of supply then
results in a reduction in demand for seafood. This could
then lead to seafood companies going out of business.
Companies greatly affected by this include southern US
fish industries like those in Georgia and Mississippi.
The reduction of seafood demand then negatively affects
the national GDP since there are fewer goods being sold
to the common public. Seafood industries that lose
popularity will increase the unemployment rate as the
layoffs would increase.
